Sunset at Sunset Cliffs

Sunset at 7:07 PM

This is the reason we built the whole evening around this time of day. Every stop before this was timed so you’d arrive here right as the light turns golden. Dramatic cliff formations, coastal arches, and unobstructed Pacific views. The trail is easy and mostly flat so you can just take it all in. If you can, arriving around 5:45 gives you time to explore, find a cozy spot, and settle in together.

You might want: A blanket or towel to sit on, and a jacket for the ocean breeze.

Parking: Free dirt lots off Ladera St and Cornish Dr. Also free street parking along Sunset Cliffs Blvd. Spots tend to fill up closer to sunset, so arriving before 6 PM is a good idea.

Coast Walk Trail & Seal Watching

2:30 to 3:30 PM

We open with this on purpose. Starting active builds natural energy between you two before you sit down to eat. This 0.6-mile cliffside path has nonstop ocean views, sea lion sounds, and kayakers below. It’s flat, easy, and the opposite of strenuous. After the trail, walk over to Children’s Pool to watch harbor seals sunbathing from the seawall above.

Free. No ticket, no time limit. Just show up and enjoy it together.

~10 min stroll through La Jolla Village to the restaurant

After working up an appetite on the Coast Walk, we sit you down at an authentic Thai spot right on La Jolla’s main street. The Pad Thai, Thai Mango Chicken, and curries are all boldly flavored with fresh ingredients. It’s the kind of local spot you wouldn’t find on your own, which is exactly why we picked it.

Timing note: They close for a break 3 to 4 PM on Mondays, then reopen at 4 PM. We timed your Coast Walk and village stroll so you arrive right when they reopen.

Monday hours: 11 AM to 3 PM, 4 PM to 9 PM.

Sunset at Scripps Park

Sunset at 7:07 PM

Everything before this was building to this moment. A 5.6-acre park on rocky bluffs right next to La Jolla Cove. The grass is perfect for settling in with gelato and watching the sky light up together. The green belvedere huts are great for photos. You have almost two hours here with no rush and no agenda.

Free. Ocean sounds, open space, and nowhere you need to be.
