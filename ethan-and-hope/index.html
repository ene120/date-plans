12 acre bayside park with harbor views and Coronado Bridge

A stunning 12 acre bayside park right on the harbor. Walk along the promenade with panoramic views of the Coronado Bridge, the USS Midway, and sailboats dotting the bay. The interactive fountains, lush gardens, and wide open lawns make it one of the most photogenic spots in San Diego. Late March/early April sunset is around 7:15 PM, so you’ll catch the beginning of golden hour while you stroll.

Free admission. Open sunrise to sunset. Perfect for photos and a relaxed start to the evening.

Michelin Bib Gourmand, 1920s warehouse with open kitchen

A Michelin Bib Gourmand restaurant set in a beautifully restored 1920s warehouse with soaring ceilings, an open kitchen, and industrial chic vibes. The menu is New American with seasonal, locally sourced ingredients and creative presentations that make every dish feel special. The atmosphere strikes the perfect balance: elevated enough to feel like an occasion, relaxed enough to be yourselves.

Saturday hours: 5:00 to 10:00 PM. Reservations strongly recommended. Book on OpenTable for 6:00 PM.

Art deco rooftop with Little Italy skyline views

One of San Diego’s most stunning rooftop bars. The open air space features art deco design, dramatic murals, and sweeping views of the Little Italy skyline. The cocktail program is world class: think tableside martinis, hand-carved ice, and drinks that feel like an experience. It’s the kind of place that turns a great night into an unforgettable one.

Saturday rooftop hours: 5:00 PM to close. No reservations for the rooftop, first come first served. Arriving around 8:00 PM on a Saturday, you should be able to grab seats.

Tip: Bring a light jacket. The rooftop can get breezy after sunset, especially in early April.
