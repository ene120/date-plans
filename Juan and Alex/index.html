1

Mission Hills Park (Pioneer Park) Stroll

1521 Washington Pl, San Diego, CA 92103

Google Maps Apple Maps Park Info
scenic • calm • old-San-Diego

You start with a slow wander under big trees and open lawn space. It feels like a small “reset” before dinner, and it scratches that spontaneous-overlook-date feeling without needing a big drive. The park is available from 6 AM to dusk.

What you do: Take one loop, then sit for a few minutes and people-watch.

✨ You might like:

  • Bringing something small to share (sparkling water, a little snack) so it feels like a mini picnic without effort.
📋 Backup Plan

Keep it even simpler and do a short neighborhood walk around Washington St instead, then head straight to dinner early. Backup exists so the start stays easy even if you want less walking.

2

Dinner at Kous Kous Moroccan Bistro

3940 Fourth Ave #110, San Diego, CA 92103

(619) 295-5560

Google Maps Apple Maps Call Menu Reserve on OpenTable
intimate • warm-lit • “new experience”

This is your “we got ready” anchor. Moroccan food gives you bold, memorable flavors without stepping into the sweet-sauce zone you dislike. OpenTable is the move here so dinner is locked in.

What you do: Share one starter, then each pick a main that fits your lane (savory-forward or adventurous), and finish with mint tea so you don't feel rushed heading upstairs after.

🍽 What to Order

Two routes tuned to your tastes — overlap a bit because you both like flavorful, memorable meals.

✨ Alex’s Route — Savory, Not Sweet-Forward
  • Chicken tagine with olives and lemon
  • Harira soup (hearty, comforting starter)
  • Chicken or shrimp kabobs (simple, flavorful, reliable)

Clean, savory, no sweet-sauce territory.

🔥 Juan’s Route — A Little Bolder, Memorable
  • Lamb tagine with honey, nuts, and fruit (the “wow” dish)
  • Chicken or shrimp b'stila in phyllo (unique, date-night fun)
  • Couscous main (classic centerpiece, easy to share)

Bold, adventurous, and shareable.

📝 Reservation Note

Reserve on OpenTable so it doesn't turn into a wait. This keeps the dinner locked in and stress-free.

📋 Backup Plan

Parma Cucina Italiana (Hillcrest) is open Sundays and takes reservations, so you still get a cozy date-night dinner without gambling on walk-in timing. Backup exists to keep dinner stress-free if your preferred slot disappears.
